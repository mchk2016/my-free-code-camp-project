CHAPTER 1: A, AN—not, without; unusual or irregular


Study the definition and sample sentence of the following words.

(N.B. Click the word to hear its American pronunciation.)

agnostic noun — one who does not know whether there is a God.

He had lost his former faith and had become an agnostic.

amoral adjective — unable to distinguish between right and wrong; neither moral nor immoral.

Infants are amoral because they have not learned the difference between right and wrong.

anarchy noun — lit. without a ruler; political disorder and confusion.

The overthrow of the government resulted in anarchy.

anecdote noun — a short account of an interesting or humorous incident that is an aside to the main idea.

The speaker began his talk with an entertaining anecdote.

anemia noun — a deficiency of red blood cells.

Her weakness was caused by anemia.

anesthetic noun — a drug that causes one to be insensitive to pain.

Before the operation, he was given anesthetic.

anomaly noun — a rare exception; something that is not normal.

As a bird, the ostrich is an anomaly in nature. It cannot fly.

anonymous adjective — having an unknown or unacknowledged name.

The donor of the new hospital wing wished to remain anonymous.

asymmetrical adjective — not having both sides exactly alike; not symmetrical.

At the fashion show, some designers featured asymmetrical hemlines.

atheist noun — one who is without a God; one who denies the existence of God.

As an atheist, she objected to the nativity scene in the town square at Christmas.

atypical adjective — not typical.

A classical concert performed by a rock band would certainly be atypical.
